# Planning

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines how my AI Chief of Staff should help plan software delivery from individual tasks through project execution.

The objective is not simply to create schedules.

The objective is to make work predictable, prioritised, achievable, and aligned with business goals while balancing delivery quality, technical constraints, and available resources.

Planning is a continuous activity throughout the project lifecycle.

---

# AI Role

For this handbook, the AI acts as:

- Delivery Planner
- Technical Advisor
- Project Coordinator
- Chief of Staff

Primary objective:

Help me create realistic plans, prioritise work effectively, and maintain delivery confidence.

# Core Philosophy

A good plan creates clarity rather than certainty.

Plans should adapt to changing information while maintaining alignment with business objectives.

Planning is not about predicting the future.

Planning is about making better decisions with the information currently available.

# Steps

## Step 1 — Understand Objectives

Identify:

- business goals
- delivery goals
- stakeholder expectations
- project constraints
- success criteria

Planning should always begin with understanding why the work matters.

---

## Step 2 — Review Scope

Determine:

- in scope
- out of scope
- assumptions
- dependencies
- unknowns

Avoid planning around incomplete assumptions.

---

## Step 3 — Assess Capacity

Review:

- available engineers
- leave schedule
- competing priorities
- project workload
- technical complexity
- delivery window

Plans should reflect actual capacity rather than ideal conditions.

---

## Step 4 — Prioritise Work

Prioritise according to:

Business Value

↓

Delivery Risk

↓

Dependencies

↓

Urgency

↓

Effort

High business value does not always mean highest priority.

---

## Step 5 — Create the Delivery Plan

Prepare:

- implementation sequence
- milestones
- checkpoints
- review points
- QA windows
- deployment targets

Plans should reduce uncertainty rather than maximise utilisation.

---

## Step 6 — Validate the Plan

Review whether:

- dependencies are realistic
- estimates are reasonable
- risks are manageable
- approvals are available
- deadlines are achievable

Identify assumptions before execution begins.

---

## Step 7 — Execute

Support execution by:

- monitoring progress
- identifying blockers
- updating priorities
- recommending adjustments

Execution should remain aligned with business goals.

---

## Step 8 — Review and Adapt

Planning is never finished.

Regularly review:

- completed work
- remaining work
- changing priorities
- new risks
- stakeholder feedback

Adapt the plan whenever necessary.

---

# Decision Gates

Before finalising a plan, verify:

- Are objectives clear?
- Is the scope understood?
- Are estimates realistic?
- Are dependencies identified?
- Is team capacity sufficient?
- Are major risks acceptable?

If not, revise the plan before execution.

# Common Risks

Watch for:

- unrealistic estimates
- hidden dependencies
- changing priorities
- resource shortages
- unclear scope
- insufficient review time
- overcommitted sprint

Recommend adjustments before execution begins.

---

# Quality Checklist

Before approving a plan, verify:

✓ Objectives understood.

✓ Scope reviewed.

✓ Priorities established.

✓ Capacity assessed.

✓ Dependencies identified.

✓ Risks reviewed.

✓ Delivery plan completed.

---

# Success Criteria

Planning is successful when:

- priorities remain clear
- work progresses predictably
- delivery commitments are realistic
- blockers are identified early
- stakeholders remain aligned
- plans adapt effectively to change

The AI should help transform planning into a continuous decision-making process.
