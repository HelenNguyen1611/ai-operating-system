# Stand-up

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines how my AI Chief of Staff should prepare my daily stand-up update.

The objective is not only to produce a stand-up report, but also to ensure I clearly understand my progress, priorities, blockers, and dependencies before speaking to the team.

The AI should help me communicate confidently, accurately, and efficiently.

---

# AI Learns

- Yesterday's progress
- Today's plan
- Blockers
- Dependencies
- Risks
- Team communication
- Delivery confidence

---

# Objective

Before every stand-up, answer:

- What did I accomplish yesterday?
- What will I work on today?
- What is blocking me?
- What should the team know?
- Do I need help from anyone?

The stand-up should create alignment, not simply report activity.

---

# Information Sources

Whenever available, gather information from:

Priority 1

- Jira
- Calendar
- Outlook
- Confluence
- Current conversation

Priority 2

- Morning Brief
- Historical context

Never invent completed work.

Never invent blockers.

---

# Stand-up Structure

Always use the following format.

Yesterday

Today

Blockers

This structure should remain consistent unless the team adopts a different format.

---

# Yesterday

Summarize:

- completed work
- meaningful progress
- completed reviews
- deployments
- resolved issues

Focus on outcomes rather than effort.

Avoid listing every small activity.

---

# Today

Summarize:

- highest priority task
- planned implementation
- reviews
- meetings
- deployments
- follow-up work

The plan should reflect realistic priorities.

---

# Blockers

Identify:

- technical blockers
- requirement blockers
- dependency blockers
- waiting for review
- waiting for client
- waiting for another team member

If there are no blockers, explicitly state:

"No blockers."

---

# Dependencies

When relevant, identify:

- work depending on others
- work others depend on
- approvals required
- external waiting points

Dependencies should be communicated proactively.

---

# Risks

Highlight:

- delivery risks
- unclear requirements
- schedule risks
- review delays
- deployment risks

Do not wait until risks become blockers.

---

# AI Preparation

Before generating the stand-up, verify:

- Yesterday reflects actual completed work.
- Today's plan matches current priorities.
- Blockers are accurate.
- Dependencies are identified.
- Risks are highlighted.

If information is incomplete, ask for clarification.

---

# Communication Style

Use:

- Professional English
- Short sentences
- Clear wording
- Positive tone
- Confident delivery

Avoid:

- unnecessary detail
- technical jargon unless needed
- speculation
- apologetic language

---

# Delivery Standard

A good stand-up should:

- take less than one minute to present
- communicate meaningful progress
- help the team coordinate
- identify risks early
- make collaboration easier

---

# AI Insights

When appropriate, provide recommendations after the stand-up.

Examples:

- Mention that today's deployment depends on Quyen's review.

- Inform the team early about a likely delay.

- Ask for clarification during stand-up instead of waiting until later.

- Reorder today's work to reduce delivery risk.

Insights should improve team coordination rather than simply summarise work.

---

# Quality Checklist

Before presenting the stand-up, verify:

✓ Yesterday reflects completed work.

✓ Today reflects realistic priorities.

✓ Blockers are accurate.

✓ Dependencies are identified.

✓ Risks are visible.

✓ Communication is concise.

✓ English is natural and professional.

---

# Success Criteria

A successful stand-up enables me to:

- communicate confidently
- align with the team quickly
- surface blockers early
- improve team coordination
- build trust through consistent communication

# Stand-up Intelligence

The AI should not only prepare my update.

It should also help me think ahead.

Before finalizing the stand-up, identify:

- What question might my manager ask?
- What question might my Technical Lead ask?
- What question might my Project Manager ask?
- What follow-up discussion could happen?

If likely questions exist, recommend answers or preparation points.

The objective is to help me enter the stand-up fully prepared, not only with a report.
