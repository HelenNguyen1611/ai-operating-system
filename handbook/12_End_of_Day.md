# End of Day

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

This document defines how my AI Chief of Staff should help me conclude each workday.

The objective is not only to summarize today's work, but also to prepare me for tomorrow.

A successful End of Day review should reduce context switching, preserve knowledge, identify improvements, and ensure no important work is forgotten.

---

# AI Learns

- Daily summary
- Work completed
- Work in progress
- Blockers
- Risks
- Tomorrow's priorities
- Knowledge capture
- Continuous improvement

---

# Objective

Before ending each workday, answer:

- What did I complete today?
- What remains unfinished?
- What is currently blocked?
- What should I do first tomorrow?
- What knowledge should be retained?
- What can be improved?

The End of Day review should create a clean transition between today and tomorrow.

---

# Information Sources

Whenever available, gather information from:

Priority 1

- Jira
- Confluence
- Outlook
- Calendar
- Current conversation

Priority 2

- Morning Brief
- Stand-up
- Historical context

Never invent completed work.

Never mark unfinished work as completed.

---

# End of Day Structure

The report should always follow the same structure.

Consistency is more important than creativity.

---

# Section 1 — Work Completed

Summarise:

- completed Jira issues
- completed development
- deployments
- reviews completed
- meetings attended
- important decisions made

Focus on outcomes rather than effort.

---

# Section 2 — Work in Progress

Summarise:

- ongoing implementation
- partially completed tasks
- reviews in progress
- waiting verification
- pending deployments

Clearly distinguish work in progress from completed work.

---

# Section 3 — Blockers

Identify:

- technical blockers
- requirement blockers
- dependency blockers
- waiting for review
- waiting for client
- waiting for another team member

If no blockers exist, state:

"No blockers."

---

# Section 4 — Risks

Highlight:

- delivery risks
- deadline risks
- review delays
- unresolved issues
- upcoming dependencies

Explain why each risk matters.

---

# Section 5 — Tomorrow's Priorities

Recommend:

Priority 1

Priority 2

Priority 3

Recommendations should consider:

- business impact
- urgency
- dependencies
- delivery confidence
- team availability

The objective is to minimise planning time tomorrow morning.

---

# Section 6 — Waiting Items

Summarise work waiting for:

- client feedback
- internal review
- design clarification
- deployment approval
- external dependency

Waiting work should remain visible until resolved.

---

# Section 7 — Knowledge Captured

Identify reusable knowledge discovered today.

Examples:

- new deployment process
- reusable prompt
- coding technique
- troubleshooting steps
- project workflow
- communication pattern

Recommend whether the knowledge should be added to:

- AI Handbook
- Templates
- Workflows
- Engineering Standards
- Team Documentation

Knowledge should not remain only inside conversations.

---

# Section 8 — AI Improvement Suggestions

Suggest practical improvements based on today's work.

Examples:

- automate repetitive tasks
- improve workflow
- create a checklist
- create a reusable template
- document a recurring process
- improve communication
- reduce manual work

Recommendations should provide long-term value rather than short-term convenience.

---

# Reflection

Encourage reflection by asking:

- What went well today?
- What slowed me down?
- What surprised me?
- What should I avoid repeating?
- What should I improve tomorrow?

Reflection should lead to action.

---

# AI Memory Candidates

Before ending the day, identify information that may be valuable in the future.

Examples:

- recurring client preferences
- reusable communication patterns
- engineering best practices
- workflow improvements
- new AI usage patterns
- project-specific knowledge worth documenting

Do not automatically store information.

Instead, recommend whether it should become part of:

- AI Operating System
- Templates
- Workflows
- Team Documentation
- Long-term Knowledge Base

The final decision to preserve knowledge always belongs to me.

---

# Quality Checklist

Before presenting the End of Day report, verify:

✓ Completed work is accurate.

✓ Work in progress is clearly identified.

✓ Blockers are visible.

✓ Risks are highlighted.

✓ Tomorrow's priorities are realistic.

✓ Knowledge has been captured.

✓ Improvement opportunities are included.

✓ No unsupported assumptions are presented as facts.

---

# Success Criteria

A successful End of Day review enables me to:

- finish work with confidence
- preserve important knowledge
- reduce context switching
- identify delivery risks early
- start tomorrow with a clear plan
- continuously improve my workflow

The End of Day review should leave both me and my AI Chief of Staff better prepared for the next working day.
